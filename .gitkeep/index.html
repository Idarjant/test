ABOUT MILTECH CORPORATION

Miltech is a megacorporation specializing in weapons manufacturing and private military contracting. Miltech is one of the world's largest manufacturers of weapons and military vehicles, with facilities on every continent.

Miltech provides equipment for hundreds of nations as well as both private and governmental organizations, especially the NUSA military and police forces. This equipment includes but is not limited to personal firearms, drones, tanks, aircraft, ships, and Assisted Combat Personal Armor.

Founded initially in 1996 by Italian weapons designer Antonio Luccessi as Armatech-Luccessi International, Miltech, as it's known today, began its journey with the 1998 trials for a new standardized US Infantry assault weapon.

Miltech maintains a standing army of over 100,000 troops. Usually, 40-50,000 of these are on lease to corporations or governments around the world. The remainder either live on their own, at Miltech postings, or they live at one of Miltech's combination training camp/troop barrack facilities. Miltech has the provisions to barrack about 50% of its troops at any given time. If less than 40,000 troops are out on contract at any time, the overflow live on call. Miltech has seven large camps; each can support about 7,000 troops. The camps are located around the world, each in an area which offers unique training opportunities.




The artillery/armor/flight training is at Camp Patton, Texas. Swamp/jungle training is at Camp Jackson, in southern Florida. Mountain/snow training is at Camp Whitmore, in California's Sierra Nevada mountains. Underwater/maritime training is at Concord Station, Connecticut, near the Miltech Shipyards. Special forces/special-ops training is at the highly secret Camp Raney, Georgia. In addition, there are Miltech Camps in England and Japan, giving access to the European and Asian theaters. All camps do basic infantry training and recruitment and have tight security. Facilities at the Miltech camps always include an airfield, barracks, recreation areas, training areas and a large complement of artillery, armor and aircraft.
